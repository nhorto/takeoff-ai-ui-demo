### Key Domain Facts for Your PAs

- **Commercial stairs (IBC):** ~7" rise / 11" tread. Industrial (OSHA): max 9.5"/min 9.5".
- **Default stringer sizes:** C10x15.3 for residential/service, C12x20.7 for wider commercial, plate (3/8"–1/2" bar) for mono-stringer ornamental.
- **Stringer length** = √(total rise² + total run²) — you already have this in the engine.
- **Picket spacing:** 4" max sphere rule.
- **ADA assist rails:** 34–38" height, 1.25–2" grip, 1.5" wall clearance, 12" extensions.
- **Typical misc labor:** 2–4× structural per ton (this is why misc is priced per-piece, not per-ton).

### Key Insight for Product Design

Misc steel is priced **per-piece / per-riser / per-SF** because labor-to-weight ratio is wildly non-linear — a 40-lb handrail post takes more shop hours than a 400-lb beam. Structural goes **$/ton** because weight correlates tightly with mill cost + predictable hours/ton. A takeoff tool that forces everything into tonnage will fail for misc. Bunéa's book is the authoritative source on this split.

## Part 4 — Tekla PowerFab Estimating

### Parametric Assemblies (the topic most relevant to your PA engine)

PowerFab PAs are formula-driven templates with **bracket-notation variables** (`[Ladder Length]`), math operators, and functions like `CEILING`. Example from the official roof-ladder docs:

```
Clips qty: CEILING([Ladder Length]/6')*2+2
Length:    [Ladder Length]+3'6"
```

### CSV Import Format (relevant to your exporter)

PowerFab maps CSV columns via **Maintenance → Estimating → Edit Input Fields**. Bluebeam integration columns: `Label, Quantity, Length, AlternateLength, Comments, Finish, Grade, Labor Code` (+ optional Category/Sub-Category). **Length units are read from the Comments field — do NOT add a field named "Comment".**
